The State of World Happiness (2021)

World Happiness Scores

The World Happiness Report is a survey conducted by the Sustainable Development Solutions Network to undertand the state of global happiness.

The world map displayed below visualises the happiness scores, also known as ladder scores, of 149 countries

Click on the filter below to view the happiness scores by region

Happiness By Region

Generally, different regions in the world have varrying happiness scores.

We observed that the North American and NZ region enjoys an average regional happiness score of 7.13 per country, the highest against other regions.

On the contrary, South Asia recorded an average regional happiness score of 4.44, making it the lowest.

Top 5 Happiest and Saddest Countries

The diagram to the left shows the top 5 happiest and saddest countries.

Finland tops the table with a happiness score of 7.8 while Afghanistan sits at the bottom with a score of 2.5.

Interestingly, all top 5 countries in this list are located in Western Europe while 4 of the bottom 5 countries are from the Sub-Saharan African region.

Select any region listed on the legend to filter the countries by region.

Happiness Over The Years

The rankings of each country based on their respective happiness scores continously changes over the years.

From 2016 to 2020, Finland consistently dominated top spot, averaging a happiness score of at least 7.6.


Toggle the slider at the bottom of the visualisation to compare between the top 5 to top 10 ranked countries or select a country on the legend to highlight its progression in rankings.

Factors Affecting Happiness

To understand what makes a country happy, we need to look at the factors affecting happiness.

With that, the happiness score of each country is calculated based on 6 key measures: Dystopia & Residual, Freedom to Make Life Choices, Generosity, Health Life Expectancy, Log GDP per Capita, Perceptions of Corruption and Social Support.
