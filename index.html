The State of World Happiness

World Happiness Scores

The World Happiness Report is a survey conducted by the Sustainable Development Solutions Network to undertand the state of global happiness.

The world map displayed on the left visualises the happiness scores, also known as ladder scores, of 149 countries in 2021

Do People Live Longer If They Are Happier?

Based on the observation of data collected in 2021, there seems to suggest a positive association between happiness and GDP per capita

Select a region on the legend to highlight only the countries within it.

World Happiness Per Region

Generally, different regions in the world have varrying happiness scores.

In 2021, we observed that the North American and NZ region enjoys an average regional happiness score of 7.13 per country, the highest against other regions.

On the contrary, South Asia recorded an average regional happiness score of 4.44, making it the lowest.

World Happiness Scores Rankings

The rankings of each country based on their respective happiness scores continously changes over the years.

From 2016 to 2020, Finland consistently dominated top spot, averaging a happiness score of at least 7.6.

Toggle the slider at the bottom of the visualisation to compare between the top 5 to top 10 ranked countries or select a country on the legend to highlight its progression in rankings.

Factors of World Happiness Scores

The happiness score of each country is calculated based on 6 key measures: Dystopia & Residual, Freedom to Make Life Choices, Generosity, Health Life Expectancy, Log GDP per Capita, Perceptions of Corruption and Social Support.
